# Evaluation Rubric

Use a 1-5 scale for each criterion.

| Criterion | 1 - Needs Review | 3 - Acceptable | 5 - Strong |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Calculation correctness | Rates, lift, p-values, or confidence intervals are missing or incorrect. | Core calculations are mostly correct for standard rows. | Calculations are correct, stable, and clearly separated from the LLM. |
| Visualization usefulness | Charts are missing, confusing, or hard to connect to the selected result. | Charts show rates and lift clearly enough for review. | Charts make group differences, segment patterns, and confidence intervals easy to interpret. |
| Memo faithfulness | Memo invents findings, recalculates statistics, or contradicts computed labels. | Memo mostly follows computed results with minor wording issues. | Memo stays grounded in computed results and clearly reflects labels, warnings, and significance. |
| Decision caution | Directional or small-sample results are overstated. | Some caution is included but could be clearer. | Warnings, sample size limits, and non-significant results are handled carefully. |
| Actionability | Output does not help an analyst decide what to report or review. | Output gives a general recommendation. | Output gives clear next steps while preserving the need for human review. |
